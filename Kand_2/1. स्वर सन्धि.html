Kāṇḍa 2: Nāda-Pravāha (नाद-प्रवाह)

Mastering the Flow of Sound

Lesson 2.1: The Union of Vowels – Svara Sandhi (स्वर सन्धि)

If Kāṇḍa 1 was about the "atoms" of sound (अक्षर), Kāṇḍa 2 is about their "chemistry"—how they combine to form molecules of meaning. We begin with vowels, the heart of every syllable.

Svara Sandhi deals with the sound changes that occur when the last vowel of one word meets the first vowel of the next. We will learn the four most essential rules today.

1. Savarṇa Dīrgha Sandhi (सवर्णदीर्घ सन्धि) - The Rule of Homorganic Lengthening

This is the simplest rule. Sa-varṇa means "of the same class" or "homorganic." When two simple vowels of the same class meet, they combine into the single long (dīrgha) version of that vowel.

अ/आ + अ/आ → आ

इ/ई + इ/ई → ई

उ/ऊ + उ/ऊ → ऊ

  • Example: न + अस्ति (na + asti, not + is) → नास्ति (nāsti, is not)
  • Example: विद्या + आलयः (vidyā + ālayaḥ, knowledge + house) → विद्यालयः (vidyālayaḥ, school)
  • Example: कवि + इन्द्रः (kavi + indraḥ, poet + lord) → कवीन्द्रः (kavīndraḥ, lord of poets)
  • Example: भानु + उदयः (bhānu + udayaḥ, sun + rising) → भानूदयः (bhānūdayaḥ, sunrise)

2. Guṇa Sandhi (गुण सन्धि) - The Rule of Qualitative Grade

This extremely common rule applies when a final अ or आ (a/ā) is followed by a simple vowel of a different class. The two vowels merge into a "Guṇa" or 'A-grade' vowel (ए, ओ).

अ/आ + इ/ई → ए (e)

अ/आ + उ/ऊ → ओ (o)

अ/आ + ऋ/ॠ → अर् (ar)

Phonetic Logic: Feel this in your mouth. 'a' is from the throat (guttural). 'i' is from the palate. Combine them, and your mouth naturally forms 'e', a guttural-palatal sound. Similarly, guttural 'a' + labial 'u' = guttural-labial 'o'.

  • Example: देव + इन्द्रः (deva + indraḥ, god + Indra) → देवेन्द्रः (devendraḥ)
  • Example: महा + ईश्वरः (mahā + īśvaraḥ, great + lord) → महेश्वरः (maheśvaraḥ)
  • Example: सूर्य + उदयः (sūrya + udayaḥ, sun + rising) → सूर्योदयः (sūryodayaḥ, sunrise)
  • Example: देव + ऋषिः (deva + ṛṣiḥ, god + sage) → देवर्षिः (devarṣiḥ)

3. Vṛddhi Sandhi (वृद्धि सन्धि) - The Rule of Incremental Grade

Vṛddhi means "growth" or "increase." This rule is the next step up from Guṇa. It applies when a final अ or आ (a/ā) is followed by a diphthong (ए, ऐ, ओ, औ). They combine into the stronger "Vṛddhi" or 'AA-grade' vowel (ऐ, औ).

अ/आ + ए/ऐ → ऐ (ai)

अ/आ + ओ/औ → औ (au)

  • Example: एक + एकम् (eka + ekam, one + one) → एकैकम् (ekaikam, one by one)
  • Example: सदा + एव (sadā + eva, always + only) → सदैव (sadaiva, always)
  • Example: जल + ओघः (jala + oghaḥ, water + flood) → जलौघः (jalaughaḥ, flood of water)

4. Yaṇ Sandhi (यण् सन्धि) - The Rule of Semi-vowel Transformation

This rule works in the opposite direction. It applies when a final vowel is NOT अ/आ (इ, उ, ऋ, ऌ), and it is followed by any dissimilar vowel. In this case, the first vowel doesn't merge, but transforms into its corresponding semi-vowel.

इ/ई → य् (y)

उ/ऊ → व् (v)

ऋ/ॠ → र् (r)

The resulting semi-vowel (य्, व्, र्) then joins with the following vowel.

  • Example: इति + आदि (iti + ādi, thus + etc.) → इत्यादि (ityādi, etcetera)
  • Example: सु + आगतम् (su + āgatam, well + come) → स्वागतम् (svāgatam, welcome)
  • Example: पितृ + आदेशः (pitṛ + ādeśaḥ, father + order) → पित्रादेशः (pitrādeśaḥ, father's order)
  • Example: प्रति + एकम् (prati + ekam, towards + one) → प्रत्येकम् (pratyekam, each one)

Deeper Concept: Speech as a Fluid, Not as Bricks

Modern Western languages, with their spaced words, often make us think of sentences as "bricks" laid side by side. Sandhi teaches us to experience language as a fluid, a flowing river. Each word modifies its shape slightly to welcome the next, creating a continuous, musical, and efficient stream of sound. This is the natural state of human speech, and Sanskrit codifies this elegant flow into a precise science.

Conclusion of Lesson 2.1: You have now learned the four pillar rules of Vowel Sandhi. Practice them until they become instinctive. Being able to spot a `Guṇa` or a `Yaṇ` sandhi in a line of text is like a musician being able to hear individual notes within a chord. It is a fundamental skill for deciphering Sanskrit.
